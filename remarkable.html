AccessiChat

Project Overview


The Product:

AccessiChat is an accommodating app dedicated to enhancing communication for individuals with disabilities. Its multifaceted features include speech-to-text conversion, editing capabilities, and scenarios to practice communicating. Users share their story through a personalized “About Me” profile and can track their communication goals and progress. Moreover, experience the joy of conversing with an endearing AI pet, housed in a delightful virtual environment with a playful store for motivation. Embrace empowerment and enjoyment with our all-inclusive platform.

Responsibilities:

Design thinking, inclusive design, collaboration, conducting interviews, forming insights, empathy mapping, prioritizing insights, generating solutions, prioritizing solutions, market research, paper and digital wire-framing, low and high fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, iterating on designs, determining information architecture.

Duration:

5 week Remarkable Designathon June 2023 - July 2023

The Goal:

AccessiChat aims to enable greater independence and participation for people with disabilities in their daily lives and communities.

The Problem:

How might we use AI to enable greater independence and participation for people with disabilities in their daily lives and in their communities?

My Role:

UX Designer with a team of two other designers leading the app from conception to delivery with double diamond design methodology. This was for the Remarkable design-athon 2023.

Collaborating as a Team

To start the project, my team and I got to know each-other’s goals, roles and skills, values, and plan roles and activities. That way, we could minimize misunderstandings and work cohesively. We named our team AccessiBuddies.

Research

In the discover phase, my team and I spent the week researching AI technologies and reaching out to people with disabilities to learn more about their conditions and what would truly benefit them.

Conducting Interviews

My team and I reached out to many people and communities to find potential interviewees. We found 3 willing participants who live with a disability and asked them open-ended questions to discover more.

Then, we voted on which “How Might We” questions we thought were most relevant to our users and problem. Usability Testing

We prepared for the usability testing by identifying risks in our product, and brainstorming the most valuable questions we can ask from that. Next, we called back the three people who participated in interviews in the beginning to receive feedback on our product.

Test Preparation: Feature Analysis, Brainstorming, and Script

Updating our Prototype After Testing Results and Feedback

We took notes, and listened to users, and:

  • Updated homepage to highlight the most relevant features: speech to text and scenario practicing
  • Updated the goal setting to include actionable steps so that people are more likely to follow through on their goals
  • Fixed confusing buttons or cues
  • Added a help section for quick information on how to use the app
  • Added prompts to guide users
  • Changed the user profile to include “I would prefer” instead of vague likes/dislikes
